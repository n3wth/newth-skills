## Tone Guidelines

### Formal Tone
- Use complete sentences and proper grammar
- Avoid contractions (don't â†’ do not)
- Use professional greetings (Dear, Sincerely)
- Maintain distance and respect
- Example: "I would appreciate your consideration of this matter."

### Professional Tone (Default)
- Balance formality with approachability
- Use standard business language
- Clear and concise
- Friendly but professional greetings (Hi, Best regards)
- Example: "I'd appreciate if you could review this by Friday."

### Casual Tone
- Conversational and friendly
- Use contractions naturally
- Informal greetings (Hey, Thanks)
- Personal touches acceptable
- Example: "Let me know what you think when you get a chance!"

## Email Types

### Cold Outreach
**Goal**: Get a response and start a conversation

Structure:
1. **Subject**: Personalized and intriguing
2. **Hook**: Specific observation or shared connection
3. **Value**: What's in it for them
4. **Ask**: Small, specific request
5. **Signature**: Professional but approachable

Key principles:
- Keep it under 150 words
- Make it about them, not you
- One clear call to action
- Easy to say yes

### Follow-up Sequence
**Email 1 (Day 0)**: Initial outreach
**Email 2 (Day 3)**: Add value or new angle
**Email 3 (Day 7)**: Final attempt, breakup email

### Meeting Request
**Components**:
- Clear purpose
- Proposed agenda
- Time options (3-4 specific slots)
- Duration estimate
- Calendar link if available

### Thank You Email
**When to send**:
- After meetings
- After interviews
- After receiving help
- Within 24 hours

**Structure**:
- Specific appreciation
- Reference key points
- Next steps if applicable

## Response Drafting

When drafting responses:

1. **Read the Original**: Understand the full context
2. **Identify the Ask**: What does the sender need?
3. **Structure Your Reply**:
   - Acknowledge their message
   - Address each point
   - Provide clear next steps
4. **Match Their Tone**: Mirror their formality level
5. **Be Timely**: Quick responses show professionalism

## Best Practices

### Subject Lines
- Be specific and actionable
- Keep under 60 characters
- Front-load important words
- Avoid spam triggers (FREE, !!!, $$$)

### Opening
- Use their name
- Reference context or connection
- Get to the point quickly

### Body
- One main idea per email
- Use bullet points for clarity
- Keep paragraphs 2-3 lines max
- White space improves readability

### Call to Action
- Be specific about what you want
- Make it easy to respond
- Provide options when asking for time

### Closing
- Match formality to relationship
- Include contact info if relevant
- Professional signature

## Workflow

1. **Understand Context**: What's the purpose of this email?
2. **Choose Template**: Select appropriate template or structure
3. **Set Tone**: Determine formality level
4. **Draft Content**: Write clear, concise copy
5. **Review Checklist**:
   - Subject line is clear and specific
   - Opening establishes context
   - Body is scannable and concise
   - Call to action is clear
   - Tone is appropriate
   - Grammar and spelling are correct
   - All necessary information included
6. **Personalize**: Add specific details
7. **Proofread**: Check for errors

## Tips for Effective Emails

- **Keep it short**: Aim for 5 sentences or less when possible
- **One topic per email**: Don't mix unrelated subjects
- **Use active voice**: "I will send" vs "It will be sent"
- **Be specific**: Dates, times, expectations
- **Proofread**: Always check before sending
- **Consider timing**: Send during business hours
- **Reply promptly**: Within 24 hours when possible
- **Use threading**: Keep related emails in one thread
